What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the sum of greenhouse gases, mainly carbon dioxide (CO₂), released directly or indirectly by an activity, organization, or individual. It is expressed in tonnes of CO₂ equivalent (tCO₂e) and includes emissions from industry, energy consumption, food production, and transportation. Reducing carbon footprints is essential to combat climate change and maintain a healthier environment.

Sustainable practices such as renewable energy use, waste reduction, and eco-friendly transportation can greatly minimize one's footprint. Governments and industries also play a crucial role in promoting clean energy policies. Each small step, from switching off lights to reducing single-use plastics, contributes to the global effort of emission reduction.
